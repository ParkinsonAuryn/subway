Effectiveness

Rafael Derolez

In terms of Intended purpose, this site is a personal portfolio perhaps to be sent away to potential employers or a steping stone for this mans portfolio.

I must say this site loads exceptionally fast which compliments his skills with web development. It's not particularly interactive which often suits a portfolio website. The links however work fine, that includes twitter. Overall I think it fits a web developers portfolio very well if he where a graphic designer I think it's too plain.

Aesthetically the web developer has a good eye for subtle things like a lighter typeface for body paragraphs. Since this website only includes one image I think its fair to say he choose a complimentary colourscheme to the blending colours he uses in his sub headings such as WHO? I think this website, particularly the scroll bar which just works with the text quite fitting to a web developers portfolio, perhaps live links to his prior projects would be appropriate as thats usually the first question a client asks of.

Saxons Place

I think its appropriate to compare two portfolio websites. This website is certainly aimed at presenting this artists illustrations over his qualifications. This is a definitive contact sheet included at the footer which means this is certainly intended for live use on the web.

If Im being honest I perceived the moving backgrounds and imagery around the subheadings as interactive buttons, for this reason I think it doesnt quite fit the intended purpose of presenting his portfolio. Upon clicking on each photo there was a lag time between a jolty larger version of the image loading which doesnt compliment the immersion the web developer initally grabs your interest with.

Aesthetically this website is gorgeous, upon deeper investigation I found some illustrations to have moving qualities to them, even if they were just shifts in colour. The type is certainly quirkey and casual which compliments this artists work. Over all fantastic theme to it.

ACCESSIBILITY

London Monaco

I think this websites colour impacts the users experience, specifically the colour combination of white and blue its complimentary to the imagery on the home page and the text. Initially I thought white text against a mountainous background looked bad but once you start scrolling a banner appears and changes the colours text to a more appropriately bold black, making a clear indication to the initially hidden user interface.

More specifically the navigation acts as a rollover to each section of a long infographic, initially its hidden which I've come to understand was a design decision to make the home page more appealing.

In terms of fonts, I find everything readable, perhaps the maps are too small for older readers which could have been considered. But considering there are nine sub headings in the banner I like the font size used for each heading. Everythings scalable too.

It seems the text and other contents are working around the background specifically, complimenting the general flow.

This website is meant to be scrolled, but if absolutely necessary the banners links can we clicked when they're somewhat hidden over the mountains.

Creative People

This website has made a very creative timeline for which the background colour changes according to the time of day you've scrolled to, light colours being early and purple being about 12:30pm. The colour change is seamless as well. The one thing I dont like though is the use of colour and styling on the links, theres very poor navigation.

More on navigation, as I said earlier the navigation is very poor and shoved in the corners of the website, both working links but only one brings you to another aspect of the page, the About section. But it does follow the same theme as the last websites where one scrolls down the entire time.

The fonts I find to be very light, the spacing is respective of readibilty on a computer screen but you have to squint to read much of it at 100% zoom, I found the previous website to be much better at presenting text.

The background has a lot going on at once, icons shift as you scroll which leads me to believe not much consideration was taken to layout the text containers about the background icons. Some icons even overlap the text.

This is very much a scroll to see important content website, I'd much prefer a dedicated banner like the last websites had.

Voice Over Internet Protocol

How it Works

Voice over IP (VoIP) is a methodology and group of technologies for the delivery of voice communications and multimedia sessions over Internet Protocol (IP) networks, such as the Internet.

Hardware requirements

IP Phones and VoIP telephone adapters connect to routers or cable modems which typically depend on the availability of mains electricity or locally generated power.[30] Some VoIP service providers use customer premises equipment (e.g., cablemodems) with battery-backed power supplies to assure uninterrupted service for up to several hours in case of local power failures. Such battery-backed devices typically are designed for use with analog handsets.

Popular software for VOIP

Alcatel, Avaya and Nortel are traditional suppliers with a heritage of legacy products that migrated to IP, they're trying to keep their existing customers in the fold by offering IP PBXes that carry VoIP. Companies like 3Com also focused heavily on hardware-based voice communications, while at the lower end of the enterprise market, you'll find organisations like Mitel, Suretel, and Panasonic.

Benefits of VOIP Over Traditional Telephony

Internet telephony does more than increase savings by lowering costs. It also enables the user to integrate software programs, such as e-mail, e-fax, and remote conferencing over the Internet via the telephone. In other words, a VoIP user can speak to somebody over the phone while accessing other applications, including the Internet simultaneously. VoIP installation requires little technical know-how. In fact, the easy installation process and mobility of the VoIP system offers a high advantage over traditional telephones. Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) technology can increase the overall productivity of an organization by allowing their employees to multi-task without interruption.

Flash Applications and Video

Adobe Flash Software and History

Adobe Flash (formerly called Macromedia Flash and Shockwave Flash) is a multimedia and software platform used for creating vector graphics, animation, browser games, rich Internet applications, desktop applications, mobile applications and mobile games. Flash displays text, vector and raster graphics to provide animations, video games and applications. It allows streaming of audio and video, and can capture mouse, keyboard, microphone and camera input.

Flash Video and Usability Issues

Flash Video is a container file format used to deliver video over the Internet using Adobe Flash Player version 6 and newer. The reliance on Adobe for decoding Flash makes its use on the World Wide Web a concern—the completeness of its public specifications are debated, and no complete implementation of Flash is publicly available in source code form with a license that permits reuse. Generally, public specifications are what makes a format re-implementable (see future proofing data storage), and reusable codebases can be ported to new platforms without the endorsement of the format creator. Flash content is usually embedded using the object or embed HTML element. A web browser that does not fully implement one of these elements displays the replacement text, if supplied by the web page. Often, a plugin is required for the browser to fully implement these elements.
